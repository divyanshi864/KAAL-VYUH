Mission Overview

KAAL-VYUH is an advanced Space Situational Awareness (SSA) platform engineered to safeguard Earth’s orbital ecosystem from escalating congestion, debris proliferation, and collision risk. With the rapid increase in satellite launches and orbital debris, Earth’s space environment is becoming increasingly congested. KAAL-VYUH addresses this critical challenge by building a real-time tracking and collision intelligence network for satellites and debris objects. KAAL-VYUH integrates open NORAD TLE data, SGP4 propagation algorithms, predictive analytics, Rule-Based AI classification, and interactive and real-time 3D visualization to create a unified orbital defense intelligence platform.

It's mission is to:

  • Enhance orbital safety through predictive collision detection.
  • Enable real-time situational awareness for operators.
  • Support sustainable and responsible space operations.
  • Democratize orbital intelligence for students and researchers.

Strategic Objectives:

  • Predict and mitigate high-risk conjunction events.
  • Deliver real-time orbital traffic intelligence dashboards.
  • Enable fuel-optimized maneuver decision support.
  • Preserve long-term orbital sustainability.
  • Provide accessible orbital analytics for academia and defense.

Core Capabilities

Real-Time Orbital Tracking: Continuous monitoring of satellites and debris objects.
Automated Conjunction Assessment: Detection of close approaches using orbital propagation.
Collision Probability Analysis: Risk modeling using closest approach distance calculations.
Rule-Based AI Classification: Categorization of objects by type, threat level, and orbital regime.
3D Interactive Visualization: Dynamic orbital dashboard for intuitive space traffic awareness.

Technical Architecture & Methodology

Data & Processing Layer

  • NORAD Two-Line Element (TLE) datasets via CelesTrak API.
  • SGP4 orbital propagator for precise position forecasting.
  • Python-based scalable backend supporting 30,000+ objects.
  • Spatial indexing & optimized conjunction filtering.

AI & Risk Modeling

  • Rule-based AI for object-type classification.
  • Collision probability modeling using closest approach distance.
  • Risk-level categorization for proactive maneuver planning.
  • Confidence interval estimation for uncertainty awareness.

Visualization & Dashboard

  • Interactive 3D orbital environment rendering.
  • Real-time alert dashboard.
  • Dynamic orbital region filtering (LEO, MEO, GEO).
  • Student-accessible web interface.

Technologies Used:

  • NORAD TLE Data: Real-time orbital element sets for satellites and debris.
  • SGP4 Propagation Model: Industry-standard orbital mechanics computation.
  • Python Backend: Data processing, position calculation, and collision prediction.
  • Streamlit + Plotly: Interactive 3D orbital visualization dashboard.

Strategic Impacts

For Space Agencies: Enhanced mission safety and operational continuity.
For Satellite Operators: Reduced insurance costs via predictive alerts.
For Defense Organizations: Strategic orbital asset awareness and threat intelligence.
For Sustainability: Supports long-term debris mitigation and orbital preservation.

Future Vision

KAAL-VYUH aims to expand beyond Low Earth Orbit (LEO) into Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) and Geostationary Orbit (GEO) environments.

Planned advancements include:
  • AI-driven fuel-optimized collision avoidance maneuver planning
  • Autonomous orbital defense intelligence systems
  • Enhanced uncertainty modeling and confidence interval display
  • Global student-accessible orbital analytics portal
KAAL-VYUH aims to evolve into a comprehensive orbital intelligence ecosystem capable of supporting both civilian and defense space operations globally.
